Blurring Angles


Click on canvas to toggle iteration.

Every pixel holds a point on the unit circle (mapped onto the circle inscribed in the square red[0,1] x green[0,1]) and on every step of the simulation, you average the point with its neighbors.

If every pixel held an arbitrary point in the plane, this would just be a blur. But if we average a set of points on the unit circle, and then normalize to get back onto the unit circle, we end up with "clockwise" and "counterclockwise" singularities, which attract one another and annihilate.
