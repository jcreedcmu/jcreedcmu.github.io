1. The Confluence

highly functional
The three languid rivers (from west to east) Gaio, Yanda, and Rak come together in Serkaioba Bay.
The natural harbor formed by Serkaioba Bay is essentially the reason Serkaioba exists. It provides protection from attackers, stabilizes the ket, and makes shipping convenient.

2. Modular Housing

functional
The commonest form of houses in Serkaioba (and indeed across Arkeny) have many replaceable parts: entire wall sections are standardized, and though they are made of cheap materials, and break easily, and are easily stained or lose their color over time, they are cheap to replace. Bathrooms fixtures are single pieces that can be efficiently cleaned or replaced without very specialized tools. Because of this, the chief differentiator of houses is color and pattern. Some colors are scarce (either artificially due to corporate and legal constraints, or naturally, because some vibrant dyes are more expensive to manufacture) and therefore indicators of status.

3. Kelek № 3 Food Hall

highly functional
The pay-by-weight food hall is the source of the typical working lunch in Serkaioba; all classes eat here side-by-side, office managers and construction laborers alike. The reasonable-person-principle of the thing is that people promise implicitly not to game the different costs of different foods, but in point of fact the foods available (mostly stews of various sorts, which use meat only for flavoring and not for the main bulk, with rice and potatoes and rangal for sides) tend to have the same calorie density and roughly the same cost per pound anyhow. There are some status games played by the beers and liquors people bring with them — of exotic, hard-to-procure hot sauces — but generally it is an efficient and equitable system for feeding the downtown throngs.
The breakfast offerings, while somewhat less attended, are also delicious: pepper eggs, soft sweet rice cakes, seasonal fruit, and fish.

4. Serkaioba Gardens

highly functional
A sprawling and sumptuous public park. Once the communal holding of the Keru, the brick wall surrounding it was smashed apart in various sections during the anti-oligarch riots of 2988. Only one small piece of it remains, now under glass. Shallow ponds have been built in the shape of calligraphically rendered symbols. There are fruit and berry trees, and a variety of spice-bushes. Park-goers play sports, stroll, and lounge on artificial sand dunes by the central lake.

5. Staircases

functional
Serkaioba is quite hilly closer to the rivers, and staircases snake up and down the cliffs. Some houses have no direct access to roads, and from them a modest climb (or descent) is required to reach one.
Their steps are often made from the same modular and colorful materials as are used in buildings. Sometimes instead wood, or compressed discarded inkfruit husks. The railings of those that have been built or rebuilt recently are more simple and functional, but ornate metal decorations can be found closer to the old center of the city.

6. The Motif of Circles

functional
Windows are more often circular than rectangular in serkaoiba. They are more expensive to manufacture, but are considered aesthetically superior. Circles abound in the visual grammar of the city. Roundabouts govern traffic. Osculating curves arising from arrangements of circles occur in logos and flags. Doorways are topped with circular arches. Cakes, breads, and other foods are cut into circular shapes. Coliseums and theaters are round.

7. Gaio Bridge 6

dysfunctional
The GB6 famously collapsed in 3189, during rush hour, killing dozens of commuters. There has been conspiracy-minded speculation as to whether it failure was caused by sabotage. Official accounts blame an unanticipated chemical reaction. A thick covering of yellow Ketepmorynot mold had grown over its piers shortly after its construction. The piers were unusually made of vlorinite imported from the north, which seemed to catalyze the mold’s growth. Over time the waste products of the mold invisibly weakened the piers, and even the ones farther from the center of the river (where the failure first occurred) were revealed by later testing to be very close to failure.

8. Public Transportation

functional
Although car ownership is not uncommon, the intracity train system is robust enough to satisfy the needs of many commuters. The majority of it is elevated, and trains cross the rivers on most of the same bridges that cars, bicycles, and pedestrians do. Serkaiobans tend to be pragmatic rather than ideological about transportation choices: different distances make different modes appropriate. Some residents have greater or lesser need for route consistency.
Few complain about the taxes required to support universal fareless entry, and the few that do are mostly pengerra anyway, whose opinions can be disregarded. Let them look at the spreadsheets themselves and see that this is the most cost-effective compromise. The train routes to other cites is urrakeny could use some improvement, true, and above all it would be nicer if there were greater integration between them and the S-trains — the tracks are compatible but politics is an obstacle — but nothing in life is ever finished sooner than a hundred lifetimes.

9. The Red Wuchka

highly dysfunctional
Of all the twelve screaming-houses in serkaioba, the red is the most tragic. It is named not for the color of its tilework — which is mostly white and blue, with occasional decorations of green and charcoal — but for the murders of 3147 that haunt its domes and passages. Yailo has forsaken this place. The common understanding is that the insult that drove away the protecing spirit occurred much earlier, in the middle of the 3120s, when the enemies of the temple were allowed to do business within its walls. They sold disgusting foods, blasphemous tonics.
The sessions in the Wuchka lost their harmony after that time. Those in attendance did not find themselves cleansed of their unruly feelings — instead the feelings were amplified. The shouts and screams were no longer music. They became anger and discord. Dances became fights. Yailo died there — transformed into Radrem, the headless winged talon, who tore flesh.

10. Principal Station

highly functional
The name of the most central train station in serkaioba is, in a way, a pun: on the one hand, it is clearly the principal station, with at least twice as many connected lines, daily passengers, and pec flowing through its retail concessions as the next busiest station in the region. On the other hand, its current name is a compromise: it used to be that it was renamed after every new principal was elected. In early 3167, it was still known as Yelechku Station, after the outgoing Principal Adrim Yelechku. After the Table Affair ended his already rocky political career for good, the incoming Principal Riaic fulfilled her campaign promise of “selfless governance” in, among other ways, breaking tradition and renaming it generically. Principals following her have since made the same decision.
